Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

MOUNTAIN POSE (TADASANA) :

The Mountain Pose, or Tadasana, serves as the cornerstone of all standing yoga postures. This seemingly simple pose requires one to stand tall with feet together, grounding through the feet while engaging the leg muscles. The pelvis is neutral, the spine elongated, and shoulders are relaxed away from the ears. Arms can rest at the sides or reach upward. This pose promotes better posture and balance by enhancing body awareness and alignment. Practicing Tadasana regularly helps improve focus, reduce stress, and increase overall stability, making it an essential pose in any yoga routine.

Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

DOWNWARD DOG (ADHO MUKHA SVANASANA) :

Downward Dog, or Adho Mukha Svanasana, is a popular yoga pose that resembles an inverted "V." This pose involves standing on all fours, lifting the hips towards the sky, and straightening the arms and legs. It stretches the spine, hamstrings, and calves, while strengthening the arms and shoulders. The pose also improves blood circulation and calms the mind, making it an excellent choice for relieving stress and fatigue. Practicing Downward Dog regularly can enhance flexibility, balance, and overall body awareness. Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)

WARRIOR I (VIRABHADRASANA) :

Virabhadrasana, or Warrior Pose, is a foundational yoga stance that embodies strength, courage, and balance. Named after the mythical warrior Virabhadra, this pose strengthens the legs, hips, and core while opening the chest and shoulders. Standing with feet wide apart, the back foot turned slightly inward, and the front foot facing forward, the practitioner extends arms outward and engages the core. This powerful pose improves balance, flexibility, and posture, while also stimulating the solar plexus chakra, promoting confidence and inner strength. Child's Pose (Balasana)

CHILD'S POSE (BALASANA) :

Child's Pose, or Balasana, is a foundational yoga posture that promotes deep relaxation and flexibility. To practice this pose, begin by kneeling on the floor, sitting back on your heels, and gently lowering your torso forward until your forehead rests on the mat. Your arms can be extended in front of you or relaxed by your sides. This posture provides a gentle stretch to the hips, thighs, and ankles while soothing the mind and relieving stress. Child's Pose is often used as a resting position between more challenging poses in a yoga sequence, allowing practitioners to reconnect with their breath and find balance. It's beneficial for alleviating tension in the back, neck, and shoulders, making it an excellent pose for those who spend long hours sitting or standing.
